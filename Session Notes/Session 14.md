# Session 14 – *Stone and Steel*

**Season:** Late Summer, Year 651 AE  
**Locales:** Outskirts of Hýrda · Ysidor's house · Meri's training ground · Ardwin's forge · Village overlook  
**Time:** Arrival at Hýrda through the following morning

---

## 1 · Arrival at Hýrda

After weeks on the road—and a deadly encounter with a disguised bounty hunter—the party crested the final hill and found themselves at the edge of **Hýrda**, Meri's hometown. Gentle hills rolled outward beneath high mountains that Ysidor called **Hýrda's Embrace** (the Harlurians know them as the Vanguards of Light, named for the goddess Nýtes crossing into mortal form through Hieroterra).

The village was home to close to a thousand stone goliaths: simple stone buildings with thatched roofs, communal in design, nestled against the mountainside. To the east, near the local mines, sat an **encampment of roughly twenty ducal army soldiers**—not blocking the village, but positioned with unmistakable intent.

**Meri's Briefing:**
- The ducal army had arrived **three to four weeks** prior
- Their commander had approached the village; Meri told him "exactly where he could go"
- No aggressive movement yet, but Meri suspected they were waiting for **reinforcements**
- The Duke's goal: exploit the mines and build a mining town, displacing the village
- Meri and **Ardwin** (the village blacksmith) had been quietly sharpening plows and forging whatever metal they could from the mines into defensive tools

Meri's position was immovable: *"We've been here as long as the mountains have. We're not moving."*

Ysidor matched her conviction: *"We're as tough as the mountains. We're not moving at all."*

Meri punctuated this with a back-pat that knocked the wind out of him.

---

## 2 · Ysidor's Grand Castle

Meri directed the party to Ysidor's house for lodging. The village buildings were communal, open-plan stone structures—no rooms, no privacy.

Ysidor, dusting off the single table: *"Welcome to my grand castle."*

He gestured around the space with sweeping pride: *"We'll be having food here. There's also the bedroom. That's the kitchen—it's also where I relax. I pretty much do everything here in this one room."*

**Berridin** had expected something… stonier: *"I thought you guys would have more stone in a stone goliath village. Like stone furniture."*

Ysidor: *"The walls are stone. The floor is stone. I'm stone. Is that not enough stone for you?"*

After a beat: *"Well, stone furniture would not be very comfortable."*

**Ellara**, ever gracious: *"Thank you, Ysidor, for inviting us to your home. It's a little different than where I grew up, but it also feels really familiar."*

Ysidor motioned them toward the "bedroom"—the exact same area they were standing in.

Nyx put his stuff down in a corner and immediately took a nap. Ysidor, surveying his guests: *"Children."*

---

## 3 · Domestic Life in Hýrda

**Whitaker** immediately asked about a bathhouse, sauna, and masseuses. Hýrda had none.

Meri, cracking her knuckles with a resonant pop: *"I mean, I could give you a massage."*

Ysidor: *"Whitaker the Fifth—he's a bit too scrawny for your type of massage, Meri."*

Nyx, flatly: *"He would die."*

Ysidor shivered, a veteran of Meri's ministrations.

Whitaker persisted about his bad neck kink, so Meri promised to return with food and "see about your friend's massage." Ysidor wished him luck with a resigned sigh.

**Family & Culture:**
Berridin's curiosity about Ysidor's family revealed the communal goliath kinship system:
- Meri was "like a mom, but not my mom"—mentor and one of many maternal figures
- *"My dad could be any one of the men in this village"*
- The house itself had been moved stone by stone after a river flood—technically not the same house, but the same stones
- At 25, Ysidor was roughly equivalent to a human 17—too young for fatherhood by goliath standards ("usually the dads are about twice my age")

Berridin: *"That did not make sense, but I'm so intrigued."*

---

## 4 · Meri's Massage & Dinner

Meri returned carrying bundles of tubular vegetables and cured meat, dropping them on the counter with practiced ease.

**The Massage:**
Whitaker swan-dived onto the bed, clothes flying. Meri—eight feet tall, towering over the naked form—didn't blink. She found the knot in his neck and applied pure goliath force to flatten it.

Whitaker blacked out.

Meri, calling over: *"Little one. Little Hill, your friend—I think he passed out."*

Ysidor performed a quiet Lay on Hands (non-healing): *"I'm just gonna do a bit of some of the human magic I learned in the towns to make sure everything sets properly."*

The kink was completely gone. Whitaker, coming to: *"Meri, you're a miracle worker. You have magic fingers. Don't let anybody tell you otherwise."*

Meri turned to Ysidor, amused: *"Little Hill—well, there's no human magic. You've always been doing this. I taught you how to do that."*

**The Stew:**
Ysidor attempted to start the fire with two rocks, failed, then sheepishly pulled out a match: *"Actually, we have technology."*

Meri prepared a hearty late-summer stew—mineral-rich and very salty by human standards.

Whitaker: *"Dude, do you happen to have any hot sauce?"*

Nyx gave him a death glare. Meri produced a glass container. She put a tablespoon on her own food; Whitaker matched her portion.

**Con save 19:** The spiciest thing Whitaker had ever tasted, but years of poor decisions had built tolerance. His mouth survived—barely. He stopped talking entirely, making inarticulate gestures while still eating.

Ysidor added hot sauce and took a massive bite: *"It sort of numbs the tongue after a while."*

Ellara tried it. Nyx tried it (**Con 12**). Berridin cast Elementalism, conjuring buckets of water to dump on his own head, burning through spell slots fighting the pepper.

Nyx retreated to his corner with hood pulled all the way up, face soaked with tears and snot, pretending nothing was happening.

Whitaker, face nearly as purple as his shirt: *"Really clears the sinuses."*

Ellara, stone-faced: *"This isn't so bad actually. This is pretty good."*

---

## 5 · The Warning

After dinner, the mood shifted as the party delivered their intelligence to Meri.

**The Warforged Threat:**
Ysidor described the constructs they'd fought—big, metal, nearly impossible to damage. Nyx chose his words carefully:

*"I know that you are clearly incredibly powerful. These machines can kill you. I choose my words very carefully."*

He described dozens in storage at the lighthouse, ready for deployment. Meri absorbed this soberly.

*"So it seems these city folk really are up to something."*

**The Bounty:**
Nyx revealed his profession—bounty hunter, sometimes assassin—and the contract on Meri's life from a high-profile assassination board.

Meri: *"Oh, you fell for the old traveling-woman-with-her-baby trick, didn't you?"*

Ysidor: *"We still haven't found the baby."*

Whitaker: *"It was a cute baby."*

Meri was more concerned with the price on her head than the danger: *"I hope it's a good price. I'd be insulted otherwise."*

She was pragmatic about assassins—*"The trail will weed out the weak ones"*—but the warforged gave her real pause.

*"I guess they'll find out why we're stone goliaths and this mountain is our home. Hýrda's Embrace is loving, but she is also the one that embraces us at the end of time, too."*

---

## 6 · Ellara's Crisis

After Meri departed and the lights dimmed, Ellara found a spot at the edge of the room, away from the others. She whispered half-prayer, half-plea to her spirit—overwhelmed by the bounty hunter's death.

*"I don't know if what just… I've never seen somebody get killed in front of my eyes."*

**Whitaker** (Perception 17) noticed first and nudged Nyx: *"Maybe you could say something?"*

Nyx, still recovering from the hot sauce—snot everywhere, not his finest moment—cleared his face and sat beside her.

*"I'm going to go out and assume this is the first time you've seen a person die."*

Ellara, sharply: *"I have seen dead bodies before."* Then, quieter: *"But maybe not one killed in front of me."*

Nyx's response was unexpectedly tender—and visibly surprised him as the words came:

*"That's normal. That's a good thing. It means you're a good person."*

When Ellara protested that no one else seemed affected, Nyx was honest: *"The bad news is you're probably gonna see more of that. And that's the world we live in. You were very fortunate to grow up where you didn't have to see that, but all of us—we've been living it."*

He paused, then: *"We're here for you."*

He hadn't said something like that in a long time.

*"If you gotta talk about shit to help you be strong, then fine. We'll talk about it. You're gonna see more of this. And one day you might find yourself in a situation where you have to choose—you live or you die, they live or they die. Your life will change after this. There's no way around that."*

He gently patted her shoulder and went back to his corner.

Ellara, very quietly: *"Thank you."*

Whitaker, from the darkness: *"I remember the first person I saw die. Stable boy got kicked by a horse right in the face. Blood everywhere."*

Nyx: *"Hey, what?"*

Ysidor: *"No—it's not the time."*

---

## 7 · Dawn in Hýrda

Ellara's prayers that night brought no dreams—only darkness and silence. The voice did not visit. Perhaps not visiting was also a sign.

**Morning routines:**
- **Ysidor** woke before dawn, found Meri already at the training ground
- **Ellara** rose for dawn prayers at a hillside overlook facing east
- **Nyx** finished his 4-hour trance
- **Berridin** conjured a cartoonish bouncing alarm clock (Minor Illusion) to wake Whitaker, who was dead to the world

**Meri's Training:**
Meri put Ysidor through drills—a rocky montage. She wielded a wooden stick against his fully sharpened weapon, unconcerned. He was stronger than before, leveled up and road-hardened. Just as he was about to land a solid hit, she moved quick, tripped him, and pinned him with the pole to his chest.

*"Huh. A little bit better, Little Hill."*

---

## 8 · Berridin & Ellara – Morning Confessions

Berridin intercepted Ellara as she returned from prayers.

*"I didn't have much to say last night about the feelings you were experiencing. But I've also been thinking about the death of the assassin. I like to put on a bold front, but to be honest—that was also my first kill. Does it make me a bad person? Could I have done something differently?"*

Ellara: *"No one but the Light can see all the paths that could have been taken. I don't think we had much of a choice. As much as it pains me to say it, I would still want to live, and she was not going to give us that option."*

Berridin opened up further—the loss of his family as a child, the enclave he'd never truly felt part of, the disturbing mix of satisfaction and horror in delivering the killing blow.

Ellara, genuinely surprised: *"Master Berridin, forgive the rudeness, but this level of introspection was not something I expected. It is comforting to know you are more than somebody who makes light of all situations."*

She confessed her own fear: *"When I looked around and nobody seemed affected, I wondered if there was something wrong with me. So it is comforting to know I was not alone."*

Berridin pivoted to his warning about Proctor Eduard: *"I'm still trying to look out for your best interests. I don't think he would be as introspective when it comes to using people."*

Ellara stared into his eyes for an uncomfortably long time, then slightly smiled.

*"I will take your words into account. It is just—this is the only life I have known, and if I believe you, I do not know what would become of me. But I think your warning… I will think upon it more deeply than I had before."*

Berridin extended his arm to her and cast Prestidigitation, creating a path of rose petals toward the house.

Ellara: *"Baby steps, I suppose."*

---

## 9 · Council of War

With the sun up and everyone assembled—Whitaker eating cold stew straight from the pot—Meri brought Ysidor to see **Ardwin** at his forge. The massive blacksmith was already building a fire for the kiln.

Ysidor's introductions were characteristically blunt: *"There's Berridin—he's still a growing boy. There's Nyx, who's… Nyx. There's Ellara, who's very nice. And then there's Whitaker, who's also with us."*

Ardwin cut straight to business: *"So, you've had an encounter with these machinations from the city. Any weaknesses?"*

**Berridin's Briefing:**
Berridin cast Disguise Self (lab coat and spectacles), conjured a Minor Illusion whiteboard and a life-size warforged replica, and delivered a tactical presentation:

- Warforged are fast—can keep pace at a full run
- Heavy, all metal, powered by **light crystals**
- The lighthouse had been experimenting with turning **shadowed goliaths into light crystals** to power their latest models
- They feel no pain and will keep fighting until completely destroyed

Ardwin, to Meri: *"Do these little ones always do these fancy tricks?"*

Meri: *"No, I think it's just our little halfling friend."*

**The Numbers Problem:**
Nyx described the warforged vault at the lighthouse—rows upon rows of dormant units, too many to count. A head-on fight was suicide.

Ysidor: *"Why don't we just face them head on?"*

Nyx: *"Because we'll all be killed."*

Ysidor: *"Well, we didn't have Meri before."*

Nyx: *"Okay. We'll live a couple minutes longer, and then we'll be killed."*

Meri agreed—she could handle two or three, but not a force: *"There's no demoralizing a machine."*

---

## 10 · The Mine Plan

**Whitaker's Idea:**
Surprisingly, it was Whitaker who proposed the strategy that stuck: lure the enemy into the mines, then collapse the tunnels on top of them.

Nyx was visibly shocked that Whitaker produced a competent tactical idea instead of something absurd.

The plan evolved through group discussion:

1. **Steal gunpowder** from the ducal encampment (the village has none)
2. **Escort miners** into the mines to extract critical supplies before destruction
3. **Plant explosives** at key structural points
4. **Lure the soldiers** (and eventually warforged reinforcements) deep into the mines
5. **Collapse everything** with them inside

**Complications & Considerations:**
- Meri's people could field a few hundred fighters, but they weren't fully trained
- Destroying the mines would hurt the village too—but Meri reasoned they could rebuild if it drove the Duke away
- The current 20 soldiers could be dealt with first, or used as bait for the larger force
- Timing was critical: reinforcements expected in under two weeks
- **Berridin** suggested knockout gas; Whitaker thought he could make some with the right materials
- Forward scouts with sending stones would coordinate the timing

Whitaker volunteered for the gunpowder theft: *"If you act like you belong, people just think you belong. That's really the key to anything."*

**Ellara's Concern:**
*"If this plan fails, would your people be able to escape into the mountains and survive there? I fear what they would do if they capture anyone."*

Meri: *"Our people can survive in far worse than this. We came from the mountains; we can stay in the mountains. But we're not just going to move because some fancy man or woman on a throne asks us to move. No lord sitting in some city is going to tell us—who have lived here far longer—what we can or cannot do."*

Meri, surveying the party: *"Little Hill, you have some clever friends, don't you?"*

Ysidor: *"Yeah, they're great."*

Whitaker: *"You're not too bad yourself."*

Ysidor: *"Thank you, Number Eight."*

Whitaker: *"…"*

Ysidor: *"You know I was never good with numbers."*
