# Session 14 – *Stone and Steel*

**Season:** Late Summer, Year 651 AE  
**Locales:** Outskirts of Hýrda · Ysidor's house · Meri's training ground · Ardwin's forge · Village overlook  
**Time:** Arrival at Hýrda through the following morning

---

## 1 · Arrival at Hýrda

After weeks on the road—and a deadly encounter with a disguised bounty hunter—the party crested the final hill and found themselves at the edge of **Hýrda**, Meri's hometown. Gentle hills rolled outward beneath high mountains that Ysidor called **Hýrda's Embrace** (the Harlurians know them as the Vanguards of Light, named for the goddess Nýtes crossing into mortal form through Hieroterra).

The village was home to close to a thousand stone goliaths: simple stone buildings with thatched roofs, communal in design, nestled against the mountainside. To the east, near the local mines, sat an **encampment of roughly twenty ducal army soldiers**—not blocking the village, but positioned with unmistakable intent.

**Meri's Briefing:**
- The ducal army had arrived **three to four weeks** prior
- Their commander had approached the village; Meri told him "exactly where he could go"
- No aggressive movement yet, but Meri suspected they were waiting for **reinforcements**
- The Duke's goal: exploit the mines and build a mining town, displacing the village
- Meri and **Ardwin** (the village blacksmith) had been quietly sharpening plows and forging whatever metal they could from the mines into defensive tools

Meri's position was immovable: *"We've been here as long as the mountains have. We're not moving."*

Ysidor matched her conviction: *"We're as tough as the mountains. We're not moving at all."*

Meri punctuated this with a back-pat that knocked the wind out of him.

Ysidor was ready for action immediately: *"So when are we mounting this attack? When are we clearing the village of these urban dwellers?"*

Meri tempered him: *"Oh, Little Hill, you're a little eager on the violence. As Hyr teaches, we must first… violence is only in self-defense."*

She was taking her role as chieftess seriously—more measured and responsible than her naturally quick and free-spirited demeanor would suggest, prioritizing the village over impulse.

*"I guess you've all had a long journey. Little Hill, no one's used your place—there should be enough space for all of you in there."*

---

## 2 · Ysidor's Grand Castle

Meri directed the party to Ysidor's house for lodging. The village buildings were communal, open-plan stone structures—no rooms, no privacy.

Ysidor, dusting off the single table: *"Welcome to my grand castle."*

He gestured around the space with sweeping pride: *"We'll be having food here. There's also the bedroom. That's the kitchen—it's also where I relax. I pretty much do everything here in this one room."*

**Berridin** had expected something… stonier: *"I thought you guys would have more stone in a stone goliath village. Like stone furniture."*

Ysidor: *"The walls are stone. The floor is stone. I'm stone. Is that not enough stone for you?"*

After a beat: *"Well, stone furniture would not be very comfortable."*

**Ellara**, ever gracious: *"Thank you, Ysidor, for inviting us to your home. It's a little different than where I grew up, but it also feels really familiar."*

Ysidor motioned them toward the "bedroom"—the exact same area they were standing in.

Nyx put his stuff down in a corner and immediately took a nap. Ysidor, surveying his guests: *"Children."*

**The Accommodation Crisis:**
Whitaker: *"Is there an inn in town?"*

Ysidor: *"No, if we have guests they just sleep in one of the village's houses. We don't get a lot of travelers in these parts."*

A pause.

Ysidor: *"We do have a river."*

Nyx: *"You can sleep in the river. That's what I thought you meant."*

Ysidor: *"I've done that before."*

Ellara, ever practical: *"What's the average overnight temperature?"*

Whitaker: *"There's not even any trees to hang a hammock."*

Late summer in the foothills meant cool evenings and cold mountain nights—not ideal river-sleeping weather.

## 3 · Domestic Life in Hýrda

**Whitaker** immediately asked about a bathhouse, sauna, and masseuses. Hýrda had none.

Meri, cracking her knuckles with a resonant pop: *"I mean, I could give you a massage."*

Ysidor: *"Whitaker the Fifth—he's a bit too scrawny for your type of massage, Meri."*

Nyx, flatly: *"He would die."*

Meri: *"What do you mean? I give excellent massages."*

Nyx: *"Won't have a head when she's done with them."*

Ysidor shivered—he'd gotten Meri's massages before and knew he couldn't handle it.

Whitaker persisted about his bad neck kink, so Meri promised to return with food and "see about your friend's massage."

Ysidor, with a resigned sigh: *"Good luck, Number Seven."*

Whitaker: *"Thanks."*

**Family & Culture:**
Berridin's curiosity about Ysidor's family revealed the communal goliath kinship system:

*"Well, yeah, I grew up in this house, but my family is—well, you've met Meri. She's one of my moms, and also my mentor. There's also my neighbor, who's also my sister, who we share a mom—but not the mom like you would think. We just all grew up together in this village."*

*"My dad could be any one of the men in this village, and my mom is—I know who my mom is. But I also don't have a mom, but I have all the moms. Does that make sense?"*

Berridin: *"That did not make sense, but I'm so intrigued."*

Nyx kept his mouth shut.

The house itself had been moved stone by stone after a river flood: *"Technically, this isn't my home, but it is my home, because it's the same stones."*

The village's elder women all served as communal mothers—some hunted, some worked the mines. *"We had one who was a great seamstress. Didn't sew much, but when she did sew, it was really good."*

At 25, Ysidor was roughly equivalent to a human 17—too young for fatherhood by goliath standards (*"usually the dads are about twice my age"*).

## 4 · Meri's Massage & Dinner

Meri returned carrying bundles of tubular vegetables and cured meat, dropping them on the counter with practiced ease.

Ysidor, looking at Whitaker with a soft sigh: *"Yeah, he's all yours. Just—be gentle. The humans aren't as tough as us."*

Meri: *"Oh, Little Hill, it's not the first time I've met a human. I know they're a little bit more frail. But he'll be fine. Just lay down."*

Ellara noted Berridin wasn't exactly hiding his jealousy about the massage arrangement.

**The Massage:**
Whitaker swan-dived onto the bed, clothes flying. Meri—eight feet tall, towering over the naked form—didn't blink. She found the knot in his neck and applied pure goliath force to flatten it.

Whitaker blacked out.

Meri, calling over: *"Little one. Little Hill, your friend—I think he passed out."*

Ysidor performed a quiet Lay on Hands (non-healing): *"I'm just gonna do a bit of some of the human magic I learned in the towns to make sure everything sets properly."*

The kink was completely gone. Whitaker, coming to: *"Meri, you're a miracle worker. You have magic fingers. Don't let anybody tell you otherwise."*

Meri turned to Ysidor, amused: *"Little Hill—well, there's no human magic. You've always been doing this. I taught you how to do that."*

**The Stew:**
Meri: *"Lahel, did you even start the fire?"*

Ysidor grabbed two rocks: *"See, Berridin—there are stones in this village."* He flicked them together. Failed. Then sheepishly pulled out a match: *"Actually, we have technology."*

He got the built-in fire pit going. Meri prepared a hearty late-summer stew from the open shelving—no cupboards, everything exposed.

Ysidor, defensively: *"It's… open concept."*

The food was very salty and mineral-rich. Nyx was just happy to be eating: *"Yum, yum, yum."*

Whitaker: *"Dude, do you happen to have any hot sauce?"*

Nyx gave him a death glare. Whitaker nudged Berridin: *"You have some hot sauce, right? Your types carry hot sauce, right?"*

Meri returned with a glass container: *"It's not that spicy, but here."* She put a tablespoon on her own food first. Whitaker matched her portion.

**Con save 19:** The spiciest thing Whitaker had ever tasted, but years of poor decisions had built tolerance. His mouth survived—barely. He stopped talking entirely, making inarticulate gestures while still eating.

Meri, bemused: *"Oh, I guess you just don't have a goliath constitution. Little Hill was a bit like that as a kid, but even now he eats as much as me."*

Ysidor added hot sauce and took a massive bite: *"It sort of numbs the tongue after a while."*

Ellara: *"Can I try it?"* She liked to try new foods. She put a big spoonful before Ysidor could warn her to go light. Nyx tried it too (**Con 12**)—suffering in silence with a single tear rolling down his face, hood pulled up. Berridin cast Elementalism, conjuring buckets of water to dump on his own head, burning through spell slots fighting the pepper.

Whitaker, face nearly as purple as his shirt: *"Really clears the sinuses."*

Ellara, stone-faced: *"This isn't so bad actually. This is pretty good."*

Ysidor: *"It's an acquired taste."*

## 5 · The Warning

Meri had broached the subject over stew: *"Well, not that I'm going to complain that you and your friends are visiting, and I know with the army and all, but I told you—you could stay with your friends. You didn't have to come all this way. We'll handle it."*

**The Warforged Threat:**
Ysidor pushed back: *"Well, I know you can handle most battles, but we saw things that aren't human—or really living—in the city that I don't think you can handle on your own. No offense, Meri, but these were big. They were really heavy. I couldn't even push them."*

Meri: *"Well, it's still a little—I mean, you're a little hill, after all."*

Ysidor: *"Yeah, but these were bigger than a hill."*

Nyx chose his words carefully: *"Look, I know that you are clearly incredibly powerful. I think there is no—you are physically impressive. These machines can kill you. I choose my words very carefully."*

*"One of them, I'm sure you could maybe defeat in one-on-one battle. But we're talking dozens, if not tens of dozens. That's a lot of hands you're counting."*

Meri: *"Nyx, I do know how to count."*

Nyx: *"I was looking at Ysidor when I said that."*

He pressed on: *"We are here to warn you, to help you. Do not—please do not take them lightly."*

Meri: *"So, you and Little Hill have fought them?"*

Nyx: *"Yes, and we nearly died. And we had the surprise on them—and then we lost it."*

Ysidor: *"Yeah, and my hit didn't do damage whatsoever."*

Nyx: *"Ysidor attacked one head-on. Hit them in the head. And it did zero damage."*

Ysidor: *"I want to say zero damage—like, offended."*

Meri: *"So it seems these city folk really are up to something. I'm not gonna be that surprised, given the situation I had found myself in. So, if the three of you couldn't defeat them before, and there's five of you now—exactly what was your plan?"*

Ysidor: *"Well, I was sort of hoping you would have a plan?"*

**The Bounty:**
Nyx revealed his profession—bounty hunter, sometimes assassin—and explained the secret boards where high-profile jobs are posted. A contract on Meri's life had appeared.

*"They didn't say you needed to be killed—they said you need to be 'taken care of.' So whatever that means."*

Meri: *"I mean, let's be honest here, 'taking care of' means my head on a platter."*

She asked about the baby ruse. Meri: *"Oh, you fell for the old traveling-woman-with-her-baby trick, didn't you?"*

Ysidor: *"We still haven't found the baby."*

Whitaker: *"It was a cute baby."*

Meri was pragmatic: *"That's to our advantage, though. Solitary creatures—they won't want to split the money. I hope it's a good price."*

A beat.

*"I'd be insulted otherwise. If I'm getting this Duke's underwear in a wad, I really hope it's a good price."*

Nyx: *"People are dying over getting to you. Let's just say that."*

She was unconcerned about assassins—*"The trail will weed out the weak ones"*—but the warforged gave her real pause. She'd need to speak with Ardwin in the morning.

*"I guess they'll find out why we're stone goliaths and this mountain is our home. Hýrda's Embrace is loving, but she is also the one that embraces us at the end of time, too."*

**Meri's Departure:**
*"Well then, enough about that—we can speak more in the morning. The stew is about ready."*

After serving out stew and the hot sauce incident, Meri rose to leave: *"I think I'm gonna go get a rest. I need to go bother Ardwin in the morning. Little Hill, you know where to find me. If you manage to get up before the sun, we can go through your normal sparring practice. Gotta make sure you didn't grow a little weak in the city."*

Ysidor: *"I promised I kept up with practice—in different ways."*

She was already out the door.

## 6 · Ellara's Crisis

After Meri departed and the lights dimmed, Ellara found a spot at the edge of the room, away from the others. She whispered half-prayer, half-plea to her spirit—overwhelmed by the bounty hunter's death.

*"I don't know if what just… I've never seen somebody get killed in front of my eyes."*

**Whitaker** (Perception 17) noticed first and nudged Nyx: *"Maybe you could say something?"*

Nyx, still recovering from the hot sauce—snot everywhere, not his finest moment—cleared his face and sat beside her.

*"I'm going to go out and assume this is the first time you've seen a person die."*

Ellara, sharply: *"I have seen dead bodies before."* Then, quieter: *"But maybe not one killed in front of me."*

Nyx's response was unexpectedly tender—and visibly surprised him as the words came:

*"That's normal. That's a good thing. It means you're a good person."*

When Ellara protested that no one else seemed affected, Nyx was honest: *"The bad news is you're probably gonna see more of that. And that's the world we live in. You were very fortunate to grow up where you didn't have to see that, but all of us—we've been living it."*

He paused, then: *"We're here for you."*

He hadn't said something like that in a long time.

*"If you gotta talk about shit to help you be strong, then fine. We'll talk about it. You're gonna see more of this. And one day you might find yourself in a situation where you have to choose—you live or you die, they live or they die. Your life will change after this. There's no way around that."*

He gently patted her shoulder and went back to his corner.

Ellara, very quietly: *"Thank you."*

Whitaker, from the darkness: *"I remember the first person I saw die. Stable boy got kicked by a horse right in the face. Blood everywhere."*

Nyx: *"Hey, what?"*

Ysidor: *"No—it's not the time."*

Nyx, after the interruption settled: *"Look, you're a part of this team. And we need everyone to make sure that we can do this. So I can't say we won't let anything happen to you, but we'll do our damnedest."*

Ellara: *"I appreciate it. I just had never seen someone get killed. I had seen people die in front of me, but that was just the Light reclaiming them. This was different. I know there have been many diatribes about when it's justified to kill, but it's very different seeing it on a page than seeing it in front of you. I will just need to pray on this."*

## 7 · Dawn in Hýrda

Ellara's prayers that night brought no dreams—only darkness and silence. The voice did not visit. Perhaps not visiting was also a sign.

**Morning routines:**
- **Ysidor** woke before dawn, found Meri already at the training ground
- **Ellara** rose for dawn prayers at a hillside overlook facing east
- **Nyx** finished his 4-hour trance, then nudged Berridin: *"Go get Whitaker. You know him best—go."*
- **Berridin** conjured a cartoonish bouncing alarm clock (Minor Illusion) next to Whitaker's bed, who was dead to the world

**Meri's Training:**
Meri put Ysidor through drills—a rocky montage. She wielded a wooden stick against his fully sharpened weapon, unconcerned. He was stronger than before, leveled up and road-hardened. Just as he was about to land a solid hit, she moved quick, tripped him, and pinned him with the pole to his chest.

*"Huh. A little bit better, Little Hill."*

Ysidor, drenched in sweat: *"I'm glad I made some progress."*

---

## 8 · Berridin & Ellara – Morning Confessions

Berridin intercepted Ellara as she returned from prayers.

*"I didn't have much to say last night about the feelings you were experiencing. But I've also been thinking about the death of the assassin. I like to put on a bold front, but to be honest—that was also my first kill. Does it make me a bad person? Could I have done something differently?"*

Ellara: *"No one but the Light can see all the paths that could have been taken. I don't think we had much of a choice. As much as it pains me to say it, I would still want to live, and she was not going to give us that option."*

Berridin opened up further: *"It's so finite. It's such a final thing to experience. It's different when I see death around me. The others don't know about this, but I lost my entire family when I was young. I lost both my parents, I lost my village, and I fled here. I've never truly been one with that enclave. It's tough, but I want to make better decisions. I've been thinking about how I kind of just jumped up. It was both satisfying and very scary to thrust the dagger into her head."*

Ellara, genuinely surprised: *"Master Berridin, forgive the rudeness, but this level of introspection was not something I expected. It is comforting to know you are more than somebody who makes light of all situations."*

She confessed her own fear: *"When I looked around and nobody seemed affected, I wondered if there was something wrong with me. So it is comforting to know I was not alone."*

Berridin pivoted to his warning about Proctor Eduard: *"I hope you remember my warning about your mentor. I'm still trying to look out for your best interests. I know I come across as aloof and flamboyant and a little bit reckless, but I don't think he would be as introspective when it comes to using people."*

Ellara stared into his eyes for an uncomfortably long time, then slightly smiled.

*"I will take your words into account. It is just—this is the only life I have known, and if I believe you, I do not know what would become of me. But I think your warning… I will think upon it more deeply than I had before."*

Berridin extended his arm to her and cast Prestidigitation, creating a path of rose petals toward the house.

Ellara: *"Baby steps, I suppose."*

## 9 · Council of War

With the sun up and everyone assembled—Whitaker eating cold stew straight from the pot, still half-asleep—Meri pulled Ysidor up after training: *"I'm off to see Ardwin. Do you want to come, Little Hill?"*

They walked to the blacksmith where **Ardwin**—an eight-foot-tall stone goliath—was already building a fire for the kiln.

Ardwin: *"Meri, Ysidor! Back from your trip already, Ysidor?"*

Meri briefed him: *"Little Hill and his friends were mentioning it looks like there will be reinforcements—these beings that are not living, moving on their own. Him and his friends had a bit of trouble with them. I think this is gonna be a bit more serious than we thought. If these come, I don't know if the village… I think we have to be a bit more strategic because we won't be able to use force to win."*

Ardwin started walking toward Ysidor's place but it was too crowded, so Ysidor fetched his friends outside for introductions.

Ysidor's introductions were characteristically blunt: *"There's Berridin—he's still a growing boy. There's Nyx, who's… Nyx. There's Ellara, who's very nice. And then there's Whitaker, who's also with us."*

Ardwin cut straight to business: *"So, you've had an encounter with these machinations from the city. Any weaknesses?"*

**Berridin's Briefing:**
Berridin cast Disguise Self (lab coat and spectacles), conjured a Minor Illusion whiteboard and a life-size warforged replica, and delivered a tactical presentation:

- Warforged are fast—can keep pace at a full run
- Heavy, all metal, powered by **light crystals**
- The lighthouse had been experimenting with turning **shadowed goliaths into light crystals** to power their latest models
- They feel no pain and will keep fighting until completely destroyed

Ardwin, to Meri: *"Do these little ones always do these fancy tricks?"*

Meri: *"No, I think it's just our little halfling friend."*

Nyx pressed for details: *"Does it have projectile capability? Do they have hands, or are the hands the weapons?"*

Ardwin, thinking tactically: *"They would have weapons… they compensate for the weight—if you're saying this is all metal, then these are heavy. They must need a power source? You said they're autonomous, so a power source?"*

Berridin: *"What we found from our investigation in the lighthouse is that they run on some sort of light crystal source."*

Ardwin: *"Oh yeah—that captain did mention light crystals."*

Meri: *"I told you about the torchless torches in the city."*

Ardwin: *"So… do you know the duration of the power?"*

Berridin explained the darker truth: *"A typical light crystal does need recharging. But these are special. The reason they were after shadowed goliaths—the reason they had Meri—was they were trying to turn these creatures, these humanoid shadows, into light crystals, which is what's powering these latest warforged."*

Ardwin: *"So the transferring… this is fucked up. A little beyond my ken, that part, but—so this powering system somehow compensates for the weight, and you say they move normal. How many are coming? Do we know?"*

**The Numbers Problem:**
Berridin looked to Nyx. Nyx described the warforged vault at the lighthouse: *"I saw an entire area, a wing, with these warforged lining the hall. There were so many, I lost count. And they were just waiting to be deployed."*

A head-on fight was suicide.

Ysidor: *"Why don't we just face them head on?"*

Nyx: *"Because we'll all be killed."*

Ysidor: *"Well, we didn't have Meri before."*

Nyx: *"Okay. We'll live a couple minutes longer, and then we'll be killed."*

Meri agreed—she could handle two or three, but eventually would be overwhelmed.

Nyx added: *"One more thing about the warforged—they don't feel pain. Even if everything is broken, they'll keep going. There's no demoralizing a machine."*

Whitaker suggested using water against the metal constructs. Nyx actually laughed.

Berridin: *"I don't think I've ever heard Nyx laugh before."*

## 10 · The Mine Plan

**Whitaker's Idea:**
Meri laid out the dilemma—the Duke wanted the mines, and destroying them would remove the incentive but also hurt the village. Whitaker asked about fighting capability; Meri said they could round up a few hundred fighters, but they weren't fully trained.

Berridin: *"If we blew up the mine, wouldn't that devastate your village? Isn't that what you guys need?"*

Meri explained they'd need to sneak in supplies first—they could rebuild later if it drove the Duke away.

Surprisingly, it was Whitaker who proposed the strategy that stuck: lure the enemy into the mines, then collapse the tunnels on top of them. Nyx was visibly shocked that Whitaker produced a competent tactical idea instead of something absurd like the water suggestion.

Whitaker: *"I like blowing things up."*

He elaborated: Berridin could disguise himself as Meri to draw everyone into the mines, then escape. Berridin worried about who'd be quick enough—called it *"almost a suicide mission."*

Ysidor: *"I think we can do it."*

Meri thought the biggest problem was being enough bait to lure them in, but liked the general idea.

**Logistics & Complications:**
They needed explosives—the village had none.

Whitaker: *"I probably could make some bombs if I had the raw material. I'll need some nitrogen-rich fertilizer, some gunpowder."* He suggested stealing dynamite from the mine itself, then clarified: *"I'm more of an idea guy for the execution part."*

Meri realized they'd need to steal gunpowder from the ducal encampment. She asked for the party's help—her people weren't nimble enough. Nyx: *"Okay. I can help."*

The plan evolved through discussion:

1. **Steal gunpowder** from the ducal encampment
2. **Escort miners** into the mines to extract critical supplies before destruction
3. **Plant explosives** at key structural points
4. **Lure the soldiers** (and eventually warforged reinforcements) deep inside
5. **Collapse everything** on top of them

**Timing & Tactics:**
- Reinforcements expected in under two weeks—the current 20 soldiers could be dealt with first
- Whitaker suggested a dry run: lure the current soldiers into the mines, trap them deep inside, then use them as bait for the warforged main force
- Nyx cautioned: *"The issue is, I don't think we can depend on being able to keep them in the mine. So it might be an issue of speed."*
- **Berridin** suggested knockout gas; Whitaker thought he could make some with the right supplies
- Forward scouts with sending stones would coordinate timing
- Ysidor knew the mines well but couldn't stealth to save his life

**Mine Ownership:**
The village was communal, but the Duke claimed ownership under imperial law—a legal technicality the goliaths didn't recognize.

Whitaker volunteered for the gunpowder theft: *"If you act like you belong, people just think you belong. That's really the key to anything."*

**Ellara's Concern:**
*"If this plan fails, would your people be able to escape into the mountains and survive there? I fear what they would do if they capture anyone."*

Meri: *"Our people can survive in far worse than this. We came from the mountains; we can stay in the mountains. But we're not just going to move because some fancy man or woman on a throne asks us to move. No lord sitting in some city is going to tell us—who have lived here far longer—what we can or cannot do."*

Whitaker: *"Respectable. Although if there was an army at my estate's door, I would probably go join another estate."*

He mentioned having about two weeks until his first loan payment was due.

Meri, surveying the party: *"Little Hill, you have some clever friends, don't you?"*

Ysidor: *"Yeah, they're great."*

Whitaker: *"You're not too bad yourself."*

Ysidor: *"Thank you, Number Eight."*

Whitaker: *"…"*

Ysidor: *"You know I was never good with numbers."*
