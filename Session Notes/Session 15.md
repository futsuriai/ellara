# Session 15 - *Mushrooms, Moonlight, and Cages*

**Locales:** Hyrda · Ysidor's house · Woods near the mines · Ducal encampment at the cave mouth
**Time:** Planning in Hyrda -> same-day recon and poison prep -> failed assault -> imprisonment

---

## 1) The Plan for the Mines

With the ducal encampment still sitting near the cave mouth and the larger army still a week or two behind, the party spent much of the session trying to turn a desperate situation into a workable trap.

The core objective stayed the same: get **explosives** from the encampment, move them into the mine, and eventually bring the mountain down on the larger incoming force. The problem was the smaller force already present. About **twenty soldiers** occupied the hill by the cave, and if left alone they would simply join the larger army later.

The party kept circling the same question: do they try to fight the camp outright, sneak past it, or incapacitate it first?

Nyx finally reframed the problem clearly: the camp in front of them and the larger army behind it were **not the same threat on the same timeline**. The group would need to deal with the smaller force now if they wanted the later mine-collapse plan to have any chance of working.

That led them away from a straight assault and toward a nonlethal solution: **poison the encampment**, reduce the number of active enemies, then handle the survivors in open combat.

---

## 2) Poisons, Fungi, and Too Many Plans

Once the party committed to the poisoning idea, the conversation spiraled into dozens of variations.

They discussed:
- slow-acting poison in food or water
- gas or airborne delivery through vents
- poisoning drink instead of food
- using a fake surrender or celebration to make the soldiers drink together
- grabbing the guard captain, impersonating him, and forcing a toast
- simply attacking if all the clever plans kept collapsing under their own weight

Ellara and Ysidor searched the surrounding woods for useful plants and fungi while the others kept debating logistics. Ellara's knowledge of local herbs turned out to be strong enough that she could identify useful substitutions even among unfamiliar mountain plants. One especially promising option was a **mushroom-based agent** that would remain harmless until combined with alcohol, at which point it would make the victim violently ill and effectively useless.

That led to one of the session's central problems: **timing**. The encampment did not eat or drink all at once. Shifts rotated. Some guards would always remain on watch. Even a successful poisoning would leave stragglers, cave guards, and archers still active.

The group also learned that Nyx's poisoner's kit was much weaker than hoped - useful for adding poison to a weapon strike, but nowhere near enough to incapacitate a whole encampment. Witty, meanwhile, was confident that if someone gave him the right substances, he could turn them into something usable. He cheerfully transformed Ysidor's house into a field laboratory for poison and explosives.

In the end, the party abandoned the most elaborate versions of the fake-surrender plan. It sounded too suspicious, depended too much on enemy behavior they could not control, and required too many moving parts to work cleanly.

---

## 3) Recon at the Encampment

To make any poison plan work, they needed better intelligence.

Nyx and Berridin went to scout the encampment while Ellara and Ysidor continued working on the poison. The pair quickly confirmed several important details:
- the **mess hall** was the center of food preparation and dining
- the **captain** spent time there and was sometimes personally involved in the cooking
- the **storage** for supplies was harder to reach than expected
- the **cave entrance** was watched by guards and northern archers with strong lines of sight
- the camp was small enough that unfamiliar movement drew attention almost immediately

The disguise portion of the recon was mixed at best. Berridin's attempt to imitate one of the cave guards went badly enough to be unusable. Nyx's disguise worked better, but only barely: good enough to get in, not good enough to move freely without scrutiny.

Nyx pushed deeper into camp alone, pretending to be a guard who had forgotten something. The captain stopped her and corrected her like a superior officer, even joking that she had probably run into Berridin again and earned herself another week's pay deduction. That exchange told the party something important: the camp had a social rhythm and internal familiarity that made casual infiltration dangerous.

Still, the recon was a success. Nyx found the **mess hall**, confirmed where the **food was being prepared**, saw where soldiers slept, and mapped enough of the camp for the others to work from. What she did **not** manage to find were the explosives.

That left the party with a hard choice: go forward anyway with partial information, or spend even more time planning and risk losing the moment entirely.

---

## 4) The Final Approach

Eventually the group settled on a practical version of the plan.

- Ellara and Witty would prepare the poison.
- Nyx, Witty, and the others would provide ranged support from outside the encampment.
- Berridin would infiltrate alone and poison the camp.
- Once the poison took effect, the party would exploit the chaos, deal with surviving defenders, and continue toward the larger objective.

The poison itself was never going to hit everyone. The GM made that clear mechanically: even a strong result would only affect some percentage of the camp, and guards on duty or soldiers outside the meal would remain active.

Ellara and Witty collaborated on the poison prep while Berridin chose his method of entry. After some hesitation, he abandoned disguise work and instead used **Invisibility** to slip into camp with the poison in hand.

The party understood the risk. The poison would buy them a chance, not a victory.

---

## 5) The Poisoning and the Fight

The poisoning worked - but only partly.

Roughly **40%** of the banquet was affected, and the soldiers who dropped were the **weakest** ones in the camp. That meant the party still had to fight the most dangerous enemies: the archers, the captain, and the remaining active guards.

Combat immediately became messy.

Berridin committed hard, breaking from invisibility and going after the guard captain directly. He landed meaningful damage, but the captain was far sturdier than expected. Later the party would learn just how bad the numbers were: ordinary soldiers had only **16 HP**, but the **archers had 75 HP each**, and the **captain had 75 HP** as well.

The rest of the party tried to stabilize the field from multiple angles:
- **Ellara** used *Moonbeam* for area control, repeatedly trying to catch the captain and clustered enemies in its small radius.
- **Nyx** repositioned constantly, using movement and cover to get shots where possible, and at one point used her Raven Queen movement to find a better firing angle.
- **Witty** had one of the biggest moments of the fight, dropping **Acid Splash** on a cluster of four archers and hitting all of them at once.
- **Ysidor** kept contributing where he could, including landing a hit on a soldier and casting **Shield of Faith** on Berridin to keep him standing a little longer.

But the battlefield geometry worked against them. The mess hall acted like partial cover, the archers kept strong sight lines, and Ellara's position left her struggling to contribute at full effectiveness.

Once the enemy concentrated fire, things unraveled fast.

Berridin burned through **Mirror Image** under a brutal volley of archer fire. Even with Shield of Faith and defensive tricks, the pressure was too much. He was eventually dropped.

The captain then turned on Ellara. Her reactive magic lashed back and hurt him, but not enough to stop the follow-up. He cut her down as well.

At that point, the fight had turned from difficult to lost.

---

## 6) Surrender and the Cages

With Berridin and Ellara down, the enemy made the choice plain.

A guard warned the rest of the party that if anyone made another move, their friends would die.

Nyx used the sending stone to message **Meri**, giving the short, ugly version of the truth:
- the plan had failed
- they were surrounded
- there were cages in camp
- surrender was the only way to stop the wounded from being killed outright
- if they survived, they would look for an opportunity to escape later

Meri's answer was mostly cursing.

The party surrendered.

They were stripped of their weapons, armor, and belongings and thrown into **cages** with their fallen companions. Between Berridin and Leontia, the downed party members could at least be brought back up, but everyone remained imprisoned inside the encampment.

Ellara, somehow still finding the most cursed version of optimism possible, summed up the situation with perfect disastrous logic:

**Ellara:** *"Guys, guys, guys. We made it into their camp. Exactly where we wanted to be."*

One final consolation remained: because Witty had been the farthest away and least observed at the moment of surrender, there was at least a chance he managed to hide **a small item or token** before giving himself up.
