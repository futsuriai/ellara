# Session 16 — *Chains, Spiders, and Gunpowder*

**Locales:** Ducal encampment at the cave mouth · Woods west of camp · Hyrda · Ardwin's forge
**Time:** Minutes after surrender → same-day prison break → immediate rescue raid

---

## 1) The Liar’s Cages

The session picked up only minutes after the surrender. The party had been stripped of their weapons, armor, and packs, then split into **three reinforced cages** in the middle of the ducal encampment: **Ysidor alone**, **Witty with Ellara**, and **Berridin with Nyx**. Everyone had eaten a **goodberry** and was technically conscious, but only barely; Berridin and Ellara in particular looked wrecked, both stabilized at **1 HP**.

The cages were thick wood braced with metal, set directly on packed dirt. The camp’s **mess hall** sat in view, and the party could see guards moving through the open center of camp while the captain and his soldiers decided what to do with them.

Even in that condition, the group immediately started testing the limits of captivity. Ellara still had her amulet, and Berridin tried to scrounge some kind of makeshift focus from his clothing and the dirt around him. Nyx, still reeling and apologizing, focused on the practical loss that mattered most to him: **his crossbows were gone**.

Ellara then tried to heal herself without drawing attention. She attempted to be subtle, but her **disadvantaged Stealth check came up a 3**. The spell still worked and restored **14 HP**, but the guards noticed at once, rattled the cage, and warned her that there would be **no more Eulogian magic**.

The party learned a few key details while under guard:
- there were still about **a dozen active soldiers** in camp, including the captain
- the cage doors were wrapped in **chains and padlocks**, not simple latches
- Nyx, on an **Investigation 20**, spotted a **ring of keys** on one nearby guard

That was enough to confirm the obvious: escape might be possible, but only if they created an opening first.

---

## 2) The Captain’s Questions

The captain eventually came over in person, keeping his distance from the bars. He had already searched the party’s belongings and found their gold, which he treated as proof that someone in the village had hired them. He openly framed the party as a desperate last-ditch strike before **reinforcements arrived in a few days**.

Witty tried to bluff their way out by leaning hard on family money and noble respectability, insisting that he and Ellara were the wrong people to have locked up. The captain was not persuaded. Ellara’s magic and the party’s attack had been seen too clearly for that story to work.

The exchange did clarify the soldiers’ assumptions:
- they believed the village was already finished once backup arrived
- they saw the party as agents of that resistance
- they were particularly suspicious of Ellara because several soldiers had fallen ill after the poisoned meal

When the captain decided some of his troops needed healing, he pulled Ellara from the cage and had a lieutenant march her to a nearby tent.

---

## 3) Ellara’s Escape

Inside the tent were **four poisoned soldiers** and a single lieutenant with the keys. Ellara realized they were not actually wounded, merely sick from the poison, and after some hesitation she used **Lesser Restoration** to cure **one** of them. She made it clear she was doing no more than that, especially after being attacked herself.

On the way back out, Ellara found the one opening she was going to get.

She palmed a rock, cast **Light** on it with the glow hidden in her fist, then opened her hand directly into the lieutenant’s face at the tent exit. In the same instant, she used **Wild Shape** to become a **spider** and vanished from sight before anyone nearby understood what had happened.

The lieutenant came running back into camp furious, shouting that **“the fucking eulogian escaped!”** The camp immediately started searching. Soldiers spread through the encampment and into the perimeter, but two guards still stayed on the remaining prisoners.

Ellara, meanwhile, stayed in spider form long enough for the first wave of panic to pass, then began making her way back toward Hyrda.

---

## 4) Broken Chains

Back at the cages, the escape gave the others exactly one chance.

Witty used **Minor Illusion** to throw Ellara’s voice from a nearby tent, drawing the two guards away. With that distraction in place, Ysidor tried to break the chain on his cage. His first attempts failed. Then Berridin fed him **Bardic Inspiration**, Ysidor hit the lock with divine fire behind the blow, and on a **natural 20** he **melted and shattered the chain**.

He did not hesitate after that. Ysidor sprinted for the western side of camp, shouting back, **“I’ll be back.”**

The remaining prisoners paid for it immediately.

The captain returned, enraged, and had **Nyx, Berridin, and Witty** dragged from their cages, bound together, and chained around a pole near the medical tents. When Berridin tried to buy himself a little life back with **Healing Word**, the captain answered by striking him hard enough to erase almost all of it: Berridin dropped back to **2 HP**. After that, the guards gagged them to prevent further spellcasting.

Nyx still found a way out. Using the **Blessing of the Raven Queen**, he teleported free of the restraints and bolted for the western woods, leaving the chains behind him. That left only **Berridin and Witty** in captivity.

The captain, now fully out of patience, threatened mutilation if the prisoners kept testing him. When he tried to confirm where the escapees had gone, Witty would have answered with the worst possible choice:

**Witty:** *“Your mom’s house.”* But "luckily" he was gagged.

---

## 5) Back to Hyrda

Ysidor reached **Meri** and **Ardwin** first, breathlessly explaining that the assault had failed and the others had been captured. Nyx arrived soon after, then Ellara, and the three escapees finally had a full picture of what had happened.

The mood there was grim. Their failed attack had not only lost two party members, it had likely put the village on a much shorter clock.

Nyx, badly shaken by the entire disaster, dropped some of his usual distance and admitted a truth he had been dancing around for a while: he was not really a bounty hunter in the ordinary sense. He was an **assassin**, used to careful setups, political targets, and clean exits. Chaotic fights against a dozen armed soldiers were not what he was built for, and the session made that painfully clear.

Even so, the conclusion was obvious. If they were going to get Berridin and Witty back, it had to be **now**, before the camp fully reset and before the captain could act on the village.

Meri and Ardwin could not solve the problem for them, but they could at least help. The three escapees were given:
- spare weapons, including swords and a **shortbow for Nyx**
- enough potions to get back to full health
- no meaningful time to rest, recover spell slots, or regroup further

So the three of them turned around and went right back.

---

## 6) The Return to Camp

Nyx guided the group back in through the western side of camp, which was now much thinner on guards than before. Ellara used her familiar to scout from above, and the three made a stealth approach toward the **gray-roofed tent** in the center of camp where Nyx had seen their equipment stored.

The infiltration worked. The DC was low, but the moment mattered, and the trio passed cleanly enough to reach the tent, open the chest, and reclaim their weapons and gear.

They did **not** recover their gold. The captain still had that.

By the time they moved toward the prisoners, only **three soldiers** remained guarding Berridin and Witty on the eastern side of camp. That gave them a workable rescue plan:
- Nyx and Ellara would create the distraction
- Ysidor would come in with a horse and physically extract the prisoners
- the group would get out first and worry about fighting second

The opening ambush was rough. Nyx’s first shot, even with **Hunter’s Mark**, missed. Ellara’s first ranged attack missed too. But once the fight settled:
- Nyx landed a marked shot and kept pressure on the guards
- Ellara wounded one soldier badly, then pulled away on horseback to drag pursuit
- Ysidor charged straight to the prisoners

At the pole, Ysidor did the simplest and most Ysidor thing possible: he **ripped the pole out of the ground** and started freeing people.

Berridin, who had fully expected the others to cut their losses, could only stare at him.

**Berridin:** *“I can’t believe you guys came back for me.”*  
**Ysidor:** *“No, we would never leave a child behind.”*

Once free enough to act, Witty immediately helped justify the rescue by blasting one of the guards with **Ray of Sickness**. The attack hit on an **18** and dropped the soldier outright.

That broke the back of the rescue. The remaining guards still threatened the group, but they no longer controlled the field.

---

## 7) Gunpowder on the Way Out

At that point, the sensible play would have been to run.

Instead, the party remembered the barrels Witty had spotted earlier.

The entire reason they had come here in the first place was still sitting in camp: **explosives**. So even in the middle of a rescue, the group pivoted and tried to salvage the original objective too.

That turned the escape into a scramble:
- recover the freed prisoners
- recover the gear
- recover at least **one barrel of gunpowder**
- find a way to actually move it

Lifting the barrel outright proved awkward, especially under pressure, so the party shifted to a more practical solution with horses and nearby hauling equipment. They calmed the animals, improvised transport, and used the available horse-and-cart setup to get the barrel moving rather than trying to carry it like heroes in a storybook.

While all this was happening, the last guards hesitated. Ysidor leaned into that moment and threw his full weight behind a threat:

**Ysidor:** *“You’re on my land. Let us go, or devastation will follow.”*

His **Intimidation 16** was enough. The guards backed off just long enough for the party to finish the escape.

By the end of the session, the party had managed to get out with:
- **Berridin and Witty alive**
- their **equipment recovered**
- at least **one barrel of gunpowder** taken from camp

They did **not** get their gold back, and they still did not know what the captain was doing in response. Witty also left the encampment with an explosive surprise of his own, giving the party at least one more option if the soldiers pushed toward the village.

The session ended on that cliffhanger: the rescue had succeeded, the explosives were finally in hand, and yet the larger problem remained exactly where it had always been. If anything, it was now closer than ever to Hyrda.
